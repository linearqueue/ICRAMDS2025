Call for Papers

Submission Invitation

The International Conference on Recent Advances in Mathematics and Data Science (ICRAMDS-2025) invites researchers, scientists, students, and industry experts to submit original research contributions. This conference provides a vital platform for sharing innovative ideas and groundbreaking findings in the interconnected fields of mathematics and data science.We encourage submissions exploring applied mathematics, statistical modeling, machine learning, artificial intelligence, data analytics, and other relevant areas. Join us in advancing knowledge and shaping the future of these rapidly evolving fields.

Themes of ICRAMDS - 2025

Topics of interest cover all aspects of Mathematics and Data Science including, but not limited to:

  • Algebra, Linear Algebra and Geometry
  • Number Theory and Numerical Analysis
  • Mathematical Modeling and Fluid Dynamics
  • Blockchain Security and Applications
  • Financial Mathematics
  • Quantum Computing
  • Cyber security and Privacy
  • Software Architecture and Design Pattern
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Machine Learning and Deep Learning
  • Next Generation Network
  • Discrete Mathematics and Graph Theory
  • Fixed Point Theory and Fuzzy Mathematics
  • Cryptography and Coding Theory
  • Operations Research
  • Probability and Statistics
  • High-Performance Computing
  • Cloud Computing
  • Internet of Things
  • Information Retrieval
  • Data warehousing and Mining
  • Computer Vision and Image Processing

Full-length Paper Submission

Prospective author(s) are invited to submit the full-length paper in the given format electronically by 25 June 2025. The length of the paper shall be limited to 10 pages as per the format. One of the authors must register and present the paper for inclusion in the conference proceedings. Selected papers will be published in Scopus-indexed Springer conference proceedings after peer review. Esteemed authors are requested to visit the conference website for further details. MANIT.

Publication and Indexing

All previous editions of CVIP have been successfully published in Springer, Communications in Computer and Information Science series (CCIS). Conference Proceedings will be abstracted and indexed in DBLP, Google Scholar, EI-Compendex, Mathematical Reviews, ISI Proceedings, SCImago, and Scopus. CVIP 2025 Proceedings will also be published in scopus indexed Springer CCIS series.

Important Note

Selected papers from the conference will be published by Springer as a proceedings book volume (LNCS). Springer will conduct quality checks on the accepted papers, and only papers that pass these checks will be published. Springer Nature does not charge any money for publication of Non-Open Access content. Abstracts/extended abstracts and short papers (less than 10 pages) are not considered for publication.

*Only manuscripts below 20% similarity index (By plagiarism check) will be considered for review and further processing.
